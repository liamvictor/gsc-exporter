How to Read the Queries & Pages Analysis Report

This report builds on the Key Performance Metrics report by adding data on the diversity of your site's search appearance.

Report Columns

month
The calendar month for the data row.
clicks
The total number of clicks from Google search results.
impressions
The total number of times your site appeared in search results.
ctr
Click-Through Rate (CTR), which is Clicks / Impressions.
position
The average ranking of your site in the search results for that month.
queries
The number of unique search queries that your site appeared for. This indicates the breadth of your keyword footprint.
pages
The number of unique pages from your site that appeared in search results. This shows how much of your content is visible on Google.

How to Use This Report

In addition to general performance trends, use this report to understand the scope of your SEO efforts:

  • Keyword Reach: A growing number of unique queries suggests your site is ranking for a wider range of terms.
  • Content Visibility: An increasing number of unique pages means more of your content is being discovered and ranked by Google.
  • Correlations: Look for correlations between a drop/rise in unique queries/pages and your overall clicks and impressions.
Note: The unique query and page counts are capped at 5,000 per month due to API limitations. If you see 5,000, the actual number may be higher.
